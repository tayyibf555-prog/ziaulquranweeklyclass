Why Young Explorers

More than just a
madrasah class.

We want children to experience the mosque as a place of warmth and belonging. Young Explorers offers a calm, structured environment with clear routines and supportive relationships, helping children feel safe, motivated, and proud of their progress.

This is purposeful enrichment, designed around how children learn best — supporting emotional regulation, focus, and readiness to learn.

A Balanced Curriculum

Structured routines for the whole child.

Islamic Learning

Safar Academy & Journey to Jannah

Structured, age-appropriate curriculum with textbooks and workbooks. Reinforced by digital interactive learning using videos and quizzes.

  • Textbooks sized for ages 5–7 & 8–11
  • Interactive screen learning
  • Home subscription available

Creative Activity

Cognitive Play & Arts

Children take part in arts, crafts, colouring, and design tasks. Board games encourage teamwork and problem-solving skills.

  • Themed arts & crafts
  • Problem-solving games
  • Fresh fruit & water provided

Coordinated Sport

Table Tennis & Movement

Fast-paced, skill-based activities like table tennis support attention, executive functioning, and emotional regulation.

  • Child-friendly & full-size tables
  • Outdoor relay races & football
  • Focus on self-control, not just winning
Programme Lead

Led by Medical Experts

Young Explorers is led by a medical doctor with a special interest in child development and positive behavioural support.

This ensures high standards of care, structure, and consistency — aligning with Scotland's emphasis on holistic child development.
